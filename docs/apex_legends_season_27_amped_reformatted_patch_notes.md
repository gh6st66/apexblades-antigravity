# Apex Legends™ — Season 27: **Amped**

**Patch Notes**  
**Release Date:** November 3, 2025

---

## Season Overview

Season 27: **Amped** accelerates Apex Legends into a close-range, high-momentum meta. This update delivers major Olympus revisions, legend balance passes for Valkyrie, Rampart, and Horizon, expanded movement mechanics, weapon and attachment shakeups, a full Trident overhaul, and quality-of-life improvements across modes.

**Season themes:**
- Faster, more expressive movement
- Stronger close-range combat identity
- Reduced passive safety mechanics
- Improved audio reliability and performance telemetry
- Ranked progression that better rewards combat impact

---

- New detection model deployed in early October

## New Feature: Wishlist

Wishlist allows players to bias cosmetic drops toward preferred Legends and Weapons.

**Rules**
- Select **5 Legends** and **2 Weapons**
- Applies only to designated pack types and rarities
- Guarantees at least one Wishlist item when triggered
- Wishlist inactive if selections are fully owned
- **5 edits per season** (additional edits cost **500 Legend Tokens**)

## Gameplay Updates

### Universal Movement — Mantle Boost
- All Legends gain velocity after mantling
- Fully customizable input bindings
- HUD footprint controls available

### Heat Shields
- Removed from **all Core BR modes**
- Ring damage remains a high-risk, last-resort mechanic

## Tridents 2.0

**Core Changes**
- Fully destructible (800 HP)
- Vehicle and occupant damage handled independently
- High-speed collisions damage enemies
- Boost meter replaces single-use boost

**Movement**
- Ground jump
- Glide with steering and boosting
- Gradual altitude loss while gliding

**Recovery Systems**
- Auto-heal when undamaged
- Auto-respawn at original spawn location

## Editorial Summary

Season 27: **Amped** reinforces Apex Legends’ identity as a movement-forward, combat-driven battle royale. Ranked progression now better reflects combat contribution, movement expression is more accessible, and legacy systems like Tridents receive meaningful mechanical depth. Ongoing tuning may be required to manage close-range dominance and compounded mobility.
